===== Teşbih =====

This module answers the question: //Teşbih nedir?​//

Before you start this module have you learned about:

  * //[[:tr:ta:vol1:translate:figs_intro|Figures of Speech]]//

==== Definition ====

Teşbihte, aralarındaki ortak bir niteliğin vurgulanması yolu ile bir şey başka bir şeye benzetilir. Metafordan farklı olarak, teşbihte benzetme «gibi» edatıyla açıklanır.

==== Teşbihin Amaçları ====

  * Benzetmenin bir amacı, bilinmeyen bir şeyin bilinen bir şeye benzetilmesi yolu ile bilinmeyen şey hakkında öğretmektir.
  * Başka amacı, bir şeyin bir özelliğini vurgulamak ya da bu özelliğe aşırı derecede sahip olduğunu göstermektir.
  * Teşbihler ayrıca aklınızda bir şeyin resmini yaratmanıza, okuduğunuz şeyleri daha dolgun bir şekilde yaşamanıza yardım eder.

==== Translation Suggestions ====

  * If the image in the original is unknown to your people, use one from your own culture. Be sure that it is one that could have been used in the cultures of the Bible. We don't want to have comparisons to things that had not been invented when the books of the Bible were first written.
  * Or if the image in the original is unknown to your people, simply state the point of similarity without using an image.
  * If it would be unclear to your readers how the topic is like the image, you can state the point of similarity explicitly. However, do not do this if it was not clear to the original audience.

==== Examples from the Bible ====

>How often I wanted to gather your children together, __as a hen gathers her chicks under her wings__, but you refused! -Luke 13:34b

In this simile, Jesus compared the protection he wanted to give the people of Jerusalem to the kind of protection a hen gives to her chicks when they are in danger. It's not that he would literally gather the people underneath him, but he would protect them and they would trust him.

>For as the lightning flashes in one part of the sky and shines to the other part of the sky, so will it be when the Son of Man comes in his day. -Luke 17:24

Jesus did not say how his coming back to earth would be like when lightning flashes. But we can understand from the rest of the text that just as lighting flashes suddenly and everyone can see it, he will come suddenly and everyone will be able to see him. No one will have to be told about it.

==== Examples from English ====

>"He is __as crooked as a dog's hind leg__."

Someone might say this about a businessman who is dishonest. English speakers associate truth with being straight and dishonesty with crookedness.

>"She was __as cool as a cucumber__."

Someone might say this about a person who doesn't get emotional in a very stressful situation. English speakers associate emotion with heat and lack of emotion with coolness.
